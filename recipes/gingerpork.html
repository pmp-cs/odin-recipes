Japanese Ginger Pork

This is a popular Japanese home-cooked cuisine which use thinly sliced pork. This dish is sweet and salty. You can add some cherry tomatoes and sliced cabbage (or any vegetables that you like) beside the Japanese Ginger Pork. Eating with rice is nice.

Ingredients

  • 1 pound thinly sliced pork loin
  • 4 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons sake
  • 4 tablespoons mirin
  • 1 tablespoon grated fresh ginger root
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • (optional) 2 teaspoon sugar
  • (optional) some potato starch

Steps

  1. (optional)Coat sliced pork with potato starch.

    [Reason] Make the meat tenderer!

  2. Mix soy sauce, sake, mirin and grated fresh ginger root together.

    [Tips] If you find it not sweet enough, you can add 2 teaspoon sugar.

  3. Heat vegetable oil in a frying pan over medium heat and cook sliced pork until it changes color.

  4. Add the mixed seasoning mentioned in Step 2 in the frying pan and heat until a little of the seasoning remains.

    [Tips] I recommend using high heat, but be careful not to burn it!

[Tips] By using a 1:2:2 ratio of sake, soy sauce, and mirin, you can make any meat dish a Japanese flavor. If it is not sweet enough, adding some sugar is nice.
